About Wonders of the World

The "Seven Wonders of the World" is a list of remarkable constructions of classical antiquity.

Locations

Great Pyramid of Giza

The oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex. It was originally built as a tomb for the Pharaoh Khufu and is the only surviving wonder of the original seven.

Hanging Gardens of Babylon

A legendary garden said to have been built in the ancient city of Babylon, featuring a series of terraces, plants, and trees that appeared to be suspended in mid-air.

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

A giant seated statue of the god Zeus, created by the sculptor Phidias, and housed in the Temple of Zeus in Olympia. It was one of the most famous works of ancient Greek art.

Temple of Artemis at Ephesus

A grand temple dedicated to the Greek goddess Artemis, located in the ancient city of Ephesus. It was renowned for its size and magnificence.

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

A monumental tomb built for Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire, and his wife Artemisia II of Caria. It was adorned with elaborate sculptures and reliefs.

Colossus of Rhodes

A gigantic bronze statue of the sun god Helios, erected at the entrance of the harbor of the island of Rhodes. It was one of the tallest statues of the ancient world.

Lighthouse of Alexandria

Also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, a magnificent lighthouse on the island of Pharos, serving as a beacon of knowledge and a marvel of ancient engineering prowess.
